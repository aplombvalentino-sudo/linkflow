## CRO audit (cro skill, 5 dimensions)

1. **Value-prop clarity** — PASS. Sub-headline states category + differentiator in one
   sentence within the first viewport. 5-second test: "animated bio link with analytics."
2. **Headline** — PASS with note. "Your link is a stage." is brand-evocative, not literal;
   carried by the sub + live demo (show-don't-tell hero covers the clarity risk).
3. **CTA hierarchy** — PASS. One volt CTA per fold (nav CTA stays glass until hero exits);
   "Claim your handle" = specific value, beats "Sign up". Mini-CTAs at chapter ends only.
4. **Scannability** — PASS. ≤2 lines per copy block, mono chips index the narrative,
   alternating beats prevent wall-of-cards.
5. **Trust signals** — KNOWN GAP (pre-launch): no real usage numbers or testimonials, and
   we refuse fabricated ones (honesty rule). Demo profiles are labeled "demo profile".
   → Post-launch action: replace reel labels with real creator handles + real weekly-view
   numbers once ≥3 creators opt in; add count ("2,1k stages claimed") to the risk-reversal
   line once true.

## Loop record (v1 → v2)

- "Make your bio link unforgettable" (v1 H1) → generic-AI phrasing, killed for the
  stage metaphor that also names the product promise.
- v1 sub was 24 words with two clauses of features → cut to one clause + one payoff.
- v1 pricing header "Simple, transparent pricing" → banned boilerplate; replaced with an
  upgrade-trigger sentence ("outgrow one stage").
- v1 final CTA repeated the sub-headline → replaced with 4-word close.
- Exclamation marks found in v1 microcopy (2) → removed (tone rule).
